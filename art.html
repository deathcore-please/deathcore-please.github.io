About

Hey! I’m Shvetanshu, aka Raps. I’m a hobby artist who’s always drawn just for the joy of it — no pressure, no deadlines, just a sketchpad, some solid pens, and a lot of time to kill. My art leans towards a gothic and chaotic side: caricatures of manga panels I love, odd creatures I dream up, and the oddly meditative world of mandala patterns.

While I mostly draw for myself, I’ve also done custom pieces for friends and others who’ve stumbled upon my work. If you’d like something made — a mandala on a notebook cover, some warli painting on your guitar case, or even a unique design for a wall or wearable — I’m always open to exploring new ideas. Just hit me up via email or Instagram if you're interested in commissions or have any business enquiries. Whether you're here to browse or collaborate, thanks for stopping by :)



Sketches

As much as I love sketching, I stick to caricature drawing since I don't think I have either the patience or the talent to draw real-life sketches. These caricatures generally include my favourite manga characters, panels from said manga (or honestly any panel that I find cool on social media), and random characters/mythical creatures that come to my mind or which I come across on the net.



Back in the day I used to draw freehand without self-referencing, but I have slowly grown out of patience to redraw mistakes, and honestly avoid them altogether since I draw with solid pens. Hence I first carefully outline a rough sketch of the piece I intend to make with a pencil (since it is easier to correct) to get the dimensions right. Thereafter, when I am confident of the general dimensions, ratios and proportions by the pencil sketch, I start with filling up the piece with my pens.

Mandala

Mandala is something I have loved making since a kid. Something about its symmetry just mesmerises me into making more and more. I believe mandala does not require talent, rather it is the ultimate test of time and patience. I'm good at t probably because I don't really have much talent but do have lots of time to waste. The first one here, as you can see, is not your standard B&W mandala. As a kid I really liked messing with the oil-pastel-scratching technique. And therefore I splattered the A4 with a rainbow of oil pastel colours, which I then layered with a thick mask of black oil pastel. All that remained then was to actually carve out my mandala with a geometry-box divider. Never before had I thought that the divider would come in handy.
